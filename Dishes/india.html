Channa Masala

Ingredients
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 1 tomato, chopped
  • 1 (1 inch) piece fresh ginger, peeled and chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, chopped, or more to taste
  • 1 green chile pepper, seeded and chopped (Optional)
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 fresh bay leaves
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • ½ teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 pinch salt to taste
  • water as needed
  • 1 (15 ounce) can chickpeas
  • 1 teaspoon fresh cilantro leaves, for garnish, or more to taste

Procedure

Preparation Steps

Preparing the Chickpeas:

1. Soak the Chickpeas:
 If using dried chickpeas, rinse them thoroughly and soak them in water overnight or for at least 8 hours.
2.Boil the Chickpeas:
Drain the soaked chickpeas and rinse them again. Add them to a pressure cooker with enough water to cover them and add a pinch of salt. Cook for about 20-25 minutes (or until they are soft). If using a regular pot, it may take around 60-90 minutes. If using canned chickpeas, rinse and drain them.

Making the Masala:

3. Heat Oil:
 In a large pan or skillet, heat the oil over medium heat.
4. Add Cumin Seeds:
Once the oil is hot, add the cumin seeds and let them splutter.
5.Add Onions:
 Add the finely chopped onions and sauté until they turn golden brown.
6. Add Green Chilies and Ginger-Garlic Paste:
Add the slit green chilies and ginger-garlic paste. Sauté for a couple of minutes until the raw smell of ginger and garlic goes away.
7. Add Tomatoes:
Add the chopped tomatoes and cook until they become soft and the oil starts to separate from the masala.
8. Add Spices:
Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, coriander powder, and cumin powder. Mix well and cook for a few minutes until the spices are well incorporated.
9. Add Boiled Chickpeas:
Add the boiled chickpeas to the masala. Mix well to coat the chickpeas with the masala.
10.Add Water:
Add a cup of water (or as needed) to achieve the desired consistency. Simmer for 10-15 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together. If using amchur powder and/or kasuri methi, add them at this stage.
11. Add Garam Masala:

Sprinkle garam masala over the curry and mix well.
12.Adjust Seasoning:
Taste and adjust salt and other seasonings if needed.

Garnishing and Serving:

13.Garnish:
Garnish with fresh chopped cilantro.
14.Serve:
Serve hot with rice, roti, naan, or any Indian bread. Add a wedge of lemon on the side for a fresh tangy taste.

Enjoy your homemade Channa Masala!
