Procedure

Preparation Steps

Prepare the Cabbage:

1. Cut the Cabbage:
 Quarter the napa cabbage lengthwise, then cut the quarters into 2-inch wide pieces.
2.Salt the Cabbage:
Dissolve 1/2 cup of kosher salt in enough water to cover the cabbage in a large bowl,Submerge the cabbage in the salty water, ensuring each piece is soaked. Use a large bowl to hold the water and ensure full coverage.

Prepare the Kimchi Paste:

3.Make the Rice Flour Porridge:
 Heat over medium heat, stirring constantly, until it thickens into a smooth porridge. This takes about 5 minutes.
4. Combine the Paste Ingredients:
In a large mixing bowl, combine the cooled rice flour porridge, 1/2 to 1 cup of gochugaru, 1/4 cup fish sauce, 2 tablespoons soy sauce, 2 tablespoons sugar, minced garlic, minced ginger, and chopped onion.
5.Add Vegetables:
 Add the julienned Korean radish, carrots (if using), and scallions to the kimchi paste.
6. Add Green Chilies and Ginger-Garlic Paste:
Add the slit green chilies and ginger-garlic paste. Sauté for a couple of minutes until the raw smell of ginger and garlic goes away.
7. Add the Cabbage:
Add the chopped tomatoes and cook until they become soft and the oil starts to separate from the masala.
8. season the Salad:
Drizzle the extra virgin olive oil over the salad,Optionally, drizzle a balsamic glaze for a touch of sweetness,Sprinkle salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste.
9. Add Boiled Chickpeas:
Add the boiled chickpeas to the masala. Mix well to coat the chickpeas with the masala.
10.Add Water:
Add a cup of water (or as needed) to achieve the desired consistency. Simmer for 10-15 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together. If using amchur powder and/or kasuri methi, add them at this stage.
11. Taste and Store:

Sprinkle garam masala over the curry and mix well.
12.Adjust Seasoning:
Taste and adjust salt and other seasonings if needed.

Garnishing and Serving:

13.Garnish:
Taste the kimchi after 1-2 days. When it reaches your desired level of fermentation, seal the jars tightly and move them to the refrigerator.
14.Serve:
Serve the salad fresh at room temperature to enjoy the full flavor of the ingredients.

Enjoy your homemade Kimchi!
